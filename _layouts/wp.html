Working Papers

Credit, Bankruptcy, and Aggregate Fluctuations
Joint with Jose-Victor Rios-Rull, R&R at Review of Economic Studies. Also available as FRB Philadelphia Working Paper No.19-48 and NBER Working Paper No. 20617 (older version).
We report business cycle properties of unsecured consumer credit and bankruptcies, and investigate the underlying mechanism.
Doves for the Rich, Hawks for the Poor? Distributional Consequences of Monetary Policy
Joint with Nils Gornemann and Keith Kuester, R&R at Econometrica.
We build the first HANK model with labor market frictions and study heterogeneous welfare implications of monetary policy.
Cyclical Labor Income Risk
Joint with Vladimir Smirnyagin
We investigate how variance and skewness of labor income risks fluctuate over the business cycles.
Medical Expenses and Saving in Retirement: The Case of U.S. and Sweden
Joint with Irina A. Telyukova, R&R at American Economic Journal: Macroeconomics. Also available as FRB Minneapolis, Opportunity and Inclusive Growth Institute Working Paper No. 8.
We study how differences in medical expense risks between the U.S. and Sweden affect asset decumulation behavior of retirees.
Health-Care Reform or Labor Market Reform? A Quantitative Analysis of the Affordable Care Act
Joint with Didem Tuzemen, R&R at Review of Economic Dynamics.
We investigate health and labor market implications of the ACA, using an equilibrium model with worker and firm heterogeneity.
Literature Review of Hank Models
Joint with Yuuto Iwasaki, Nao Sudou, Fumitaka Nakamura, Bank of Japan IMES Discussion Paper, in Japanese.
We review recent trend of literature using HANK (Heterogeneous-Agent New-Keynesian) models.
Rising Earnings Instability, Portfolio Choice, and Housing Prices
I study how rising income inequality affects prices of housing and financial assets.
